How to read On-Chain Intelligence
MVRV Z-Score measures the deviation between market value and realized value (the price at which each coin last moved on-chain). Values above 7 historically mark cycle tops (overheated market). Values below 0 mark cycle bottoms (undervalued). This is the single most reliable on-chain cycle indicator.

NUPL (Net Unrealized Profit/Loss) shows the aggregate profit or loss of all coins in circulation. It maps to five emotional phases of the cycle: Capitulation (<0, green), Hope (0-0.25), Optimism (0.25-0.5), Belief (0.5-0.75), and Euphoria (>0.75, red). Euphoria historically precedes major corrections.

Fear & Greed Index is a contrarian sentiment indicator. Extreme Fear (0-25) historically correlates with buying opportunities. Extreme Greed (75-100) correlates with local tops. The index combines volatility, volume, social media, dominance, and trends.

Active Addresses measures the number of unique addresses used daily on the Bitcoin network (7-day average). It serves as a proxy for adoption and network activity. Sustained increases correlate with bull markets; declining activity signals reduced demand.

Hash Rate represents the total computational power securing the Bitcoin network. Sustained drops signal miner capitulation (often near cycle bottoms). All-time highs indicate strong miner commitment and network security.

Network Value compares Market Cap (total value) with Transaction Volume (actual usage). When market cap grows significantly faster than transaction volume, the network may be overvalued relative to its actual use. Convergence or TX volume leading signals healthy organic growth.
