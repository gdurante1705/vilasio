What is the Z-Score? The Z-Score measures how many standard deviations the current net position deviates from its 2-year historical average. Values above +2.0 indicate positioning significantly above the mean, while values below -2.0 indicate positioning significantly below the mean. Big Players (non-commercial speculators) and Commercials (dealers and hedgers) typically hold opposite net positions in the same market.
