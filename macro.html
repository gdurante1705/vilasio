How to read the Macro Regime Dashboard
The macro regime defines the directional bias before any technical setup. Understanding where we are in the economic cycle determines which asset classes to favor and which to avoid.

Macro Regime Oscillator measures the trade-off between inflation and unemployment using percent ranks over 12 monthly releases. When Unemployment rank >80 and Inflation rank <20, a dovish switch is imminent — the Fed will cut rates, dollar weakens, equities rally. When Inflation rank >80 and Unemployment rank <20, a hawkish switch is coming — rates rise, dollar strengthens, growth stocks suffer.

The 4-Regime Model classifies the cycle based on growth direction (NFP trend) and inflation direction (CPI YoY trend): Expansion (both rising — equities, commodities), Reflation (growth up, inflation down — tech, bonds), Stagnation (growth down, inflation up — gold, energy), Slowdown (both falling — bonds, cash, defensives).

Inflation Monitor tracks CPI, Core CPI, PCE, Core PCE and PPI against the Fed's 2% target. Core PCE is the Fed's preferred measure. Readings above target with rising trend = hawkish pressure. Below target with falling trend = dovish space.

Labor Market indicators confirm the growth side of the equation. Rising unemployment + falling NFP + rising jobless claims = deterioration. Strong NFP + low unemployment + falling claims = expansion. JOLTs openings measure labor demand — declining openings lead unemployment increases.
