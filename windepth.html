Why WinDepth exists

Traditional metrics don't answer
the right question.

Sharpe, Profit Factor, Expectancy — all useful. But none of them ask:
Are my profits structurally replicable, or do they depend on a few extreme events?

01 — THE PROBLEM
A system can look excellent and be fragile.
A couple of exceptional trades, a lucky period, an isolated spike. If those peaks are not replicable, the system is structurally weak — regardless of what the Sharpe Ratio says.
02 — THE PROBLEM
Profit Factor ignores internal distribution.
Two traders with identical Profit Factor can have completely different structural depth. One depends on a single outlier. The other distributes value consistently. WinDepth tells them apart.
THE SOLUTION
WinDepth measures exactly this.
It focuses exclusively on the distribution of positive R-multiples — how close your winners are to your best trade, and how stable they are around their own mean. One score. Deep insight.

Proprietary Formula

Two engines.
One metric.

WINDEPTH FORMULA
WD = 0.4 × Peak Ratio
     + 0.6 × Core Ratio
Peak Ratio = 1 − MAD(MaxR⁺ − R⁺) / MaxR⁺
Core Ratio = 1 − MAD(R⁺ − μ) / μ
Uses Mean Absolute Deviation instead of standard deviation — linear, robust, not distorted by outliers.
OUTPUT RANGE
0 → 1
Closer to 1 = structurally deeper system
Peak Ratio
Weight 0.4
1 − MAD(MaxR⁺ − R⁺) / MaxR⁺
Are your wins close to your best trade?

A high Peak Ratio means your system regularly reaches near its maximum potential. A low one means your best trade is an outlier that distorts everything below it.
Core Ratio
Weight 0.6
1 − MAD(R⁺ − μ) / μ
Are your wins stable around their average?

A high Core Ratio means your positive trades are tightly distributed — consistent, replicable, structurally solid. This is why it carries more weight.
