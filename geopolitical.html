How to read Geopolitical Risk
The GPR Index counts the share of newspaper articles discussing geopolitical tensions, threats, and acts in major global publications. It is normalized so that 100 = historical average.

Risk Levels: Low (<80) — calm period, risk-on favored. Elevated (80-120) — normal tensions, standard positioning. High (120-200) — significant stress, consider hedging. Extreme (>200) — crisis-level, strong risk-off (gold, treasuries, USD, VIX up).

Trading Implications: GPR spikes are correlated with equity drawdowns, gold rallies, and flight-to-safety in treasuries. A sustained GPR above 150 historically leads to 2-5% S&P 500 underperformance over the following quarter. The Threats sub-index captures escalation rhetoric; the Acts sub-index captures actual military or terrorist events — acts tend to have a larger and more immediate market impact.

Country-Level: Country GPR measures how often a specific country appears in geopolitical risk articles. Spikes in country GPR often precede sanctions, military action, or trade disruptions — directly impacting FX, country ETFs, and commodity flows tied to that region.
