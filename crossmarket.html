How to read the Cross-Market Dashboard
Currency Strength measures relative performance of 8 major currencies across 12 forex pairs, normalized to base 100. When a currency line rises, it is strengthening vs all others. The spread between the strongest and weakest currencies defines the carry trade opportunity.

Correlation Matrix shows 90-day rolling correlations between major asset classes. Watch for: SPX-Gold (risk regime), DXY-Gold (inverse), BTC-SPX (risk correlation). Arrows show changes from the previous 90-day window.

Sector Rotation tracks S&P 500 sector ETFs across multiple timeframes. Sectors leading on 1W but lagging on 3M may signal early rotation. In expansion: XLK, XLY lead. In slowdown: XLU, XLP, XLV lead. Energy (XLE) outperforms in inflationary regimes.

Commodity Dashboard shows performance across timeframes. Energy and industrials (copper) are highly cyclical, while gold acts as a safe haven. Divergences between correlated commodities signal opportunities.

Cointegration Charts show assets within the same cluster as % change from the start of the selected window. When one diverges from the group, it either reverts (mean reversion trade) or leads the group in its direction (breakout trade). Divergence badges appear below each chart when an asset deviates >1.5σ from the cluster average — strongest signals come above 2σ. Watch for: metals diverging from gold, Russell diverging from SPX, altcoins diverging from Bitcoin.

Value vs Growth tracks the IWD/IWF ratio alongside individual ETF prices. Rising ratio = capital rotating into value stocks (financials, energy). Falling = growth stocks (tech) outperforming. The ratio tends to rise with interest rates and fall when rates are cut.
