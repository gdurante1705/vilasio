How to read the Cross-Market Dashboard
DXY & Major Pairs shows the Dollar Index alongside major forex pairs, normalized to 100 at the start of the selected window. DXY typically moves inversely to EUR/USD (~57% of index weight). Divergences between pairs that normally move together signal regime change.

Correlation Matrix shows 90-day rolling correlations between major asset classes. Watch for: SPX-Gold (risk regime), DXY-Gold (inverse), BTC-SPX (risk correlation). Arrows show changes from the previous 90-day window.

Currency Strength measures relative performance of 8 major currencies across 12 forex pairs, normalized to base 100. When a currency line rises, it is strengthening vs all others. The spread between the strongest and weakest currencies defines the carry trade opportunity.

Sector Rotation tracks S&P 500 sector ETFs across multiple timeframes. Sectors leading on 1W but lagging on 3M may signal early rotation. In expansion: XLK, XLY lead. In slowdown: XLU, XLP, XLV lead. Energy (XLE) outperforms in inflationary regimes.

Commodity Dashboard shows performance across timeframes. Commodities correlate with the economic cycle: energy and industrials (copper) are highly cyclical, while gold acts as a safe haven. Divergences between correlated commodities signal opportunities.

Seasonality compares the average monthly return (10-year historical) with the current year. Deviations from seasonal patterns, especially when confirmed by COT positioning, create high-probability setups.

Value vs Growth tracks the IWD/IWF ratio. Rising ratio = capital rotating into value stocks (financials, energy, industrials). Falling = growth stocks (tech) outperforming. The ratio tends to rise with interest rates and fall when rates are cut.
