How to read the Cross-Market Dashboard
Interest Rate Differential (IRD) compares real yields (nominal yield minus inflation) between countries. When a country offers higher real yields, capital flows in, strengthening its currency. The spread line anticipates forex moves — when the spread diverges from the exchange rate, a correction is likely. This is the institutional framework for directional forex bias.

DXY & Major Pairs shows the Dollar Index alongside major forex pairs, normalized to 100 at the start of the selected window. This reveals relative strength and divergences. DXY typically moves inversely to EUR/USD (which represents ~57% of the index weight). When pairs that normally move together start diverging, it signals regime change or structural shifts.

Carry Trade Monitor tracks the interest rate gap between central banks and the Fed. Capital flows from low-yield to high-yield currencies (carry trade). When the Fed rate is significantly above others (positive differential), the dollar attracts carry flows. Narrowing differentials signal potential dollar weakness. Key: rapid differential changes often precede major forex moves.

Correlation Matrix shows 90-day rolling correlations between major asset classes. Positive correlation means assets move together; negative means they move opposite. Watch for: SPX-Gold (risk regime), DXY-Gold (inverse), US10Y-Gold (real yield proxy), BTC-SPX (risk correlation). Changes from the previous period (arrows) signal regime transitions.
