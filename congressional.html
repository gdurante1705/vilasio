How to read Congressional Positioning
Congressional Trades: Under the STOCK Act (2012), members of Congress must disclose stock trades within 45 days. Studies show congressional portfolios consistently outperform the market by 4-10% annually. Purchases are especially informative — when a senator buys a stock, they may have insights from committee briefings, regulatory previews, or policy changes.

Insider Trading (Form 4): Corporate insiders (CEO, CFO, directors, 10%+ shareholders) must file SEC Form 4 within 2 business days of any trade. Open market purchases are the strongest signal — insiders spending their own money means high conviction. Ignore option exercises and planned sales (Rule 10b5-1). A CEO buying $5M+ of their own stock is one of the most reliable bullish indicators.

Smart Money Overlap: When a stock shows BOTH a positive earnings surprise (PEAD signal from Tool 7) AND insider/congressional buying, it represents maximum conviction. The earnings surprise confirms fundamentals, and the insider buying confirms that those closest to the company believe in the trajectory. This is the highest-confidence setup in our system.
